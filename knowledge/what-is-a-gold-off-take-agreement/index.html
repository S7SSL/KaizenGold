What Is a Gold Off-Take Agreement?

A gold off-take agreement is a long-term contract in which a buyer commits to purchase some or all of a producer's future gold output, typically at a formula price referencing the LBMA benchmark at each delivery. The producer gains revenue certainty and often financing leverage; the buyer secures reliable physical supply without owning the mine. Off-takes are the backbone of long-term supply relationships between producers, refineries and trading houses.

What an Off-Take Agreement Contains

  • Quantity and duration — a fixed schedule (e.g. 50 kg per month for 24 months) or a share of production (e.g. 100% of mine output), with tolerance ranges.
  • Quality and form — doré within defined assay ranges, or refined bars of stated fineness.
  • Pricing formula — almost always floating: a percentage of the LBMA Gold Price fixing on or around each delivery's assay date, rather than a fixed price for the whole term.
  • Delivery basis — commonly CIF to the refinery, with the assay and settlement mechanics defined per delivery.
  • Payment security — letters of credit per shipment, and often a standby letter of credit or guarantee securing the longer-term commitment on one or both sides.
  • Failure mechanics — remedies for missed deliveries or missed payments, force majeure, and termination rights.

Why Producers Sign Off-Takes

Revenue certainty is the obvious benefit, but the deeper one is financeability. A bankable off-take with a credible buyer transforms a mine's future production into a contracted cash flow, which lenders and investors will finance. Off-take agreements are frequently a condition of project finance for new mines, and producers in need of working capital may grant off-takes in exchange for prepayment or advances.

Why Buyers Sign Them

Refineries need throughput; trading houses need reliable physical flow to supply their own customers. An off-take secures supply without the capital cost and operating risk of mine ownership. Because pricing floats with the benchmark, the buyer is not betting on the gold price — it is buying certainty of volume, often at a modest discount reflecting the producer's need for certainty and any financing provided.

Example

A mid-size producer signs a 24-month off-take with a refinery's trading arm: 40 kg of doré per month ±10%, CIF Dubai, priced at 98.75% of the LBMA PM fixing on each lot's final assay date, less scheduled refining charges. Each delivery is paid under a sight letter of credit; the buyer additionally provides a standby covering one month's average value as security for the relationship. The producer uses the contracted revenue stream to raise working capital; the refinery locks in two years of feedstock.

What to Diligence Before Signing

For buyers: the producer's actual production capacity and history, licence status, origin and responsible sourcing compliance, and realistic delivery schedules. For producers: the buyer's financial standing, the workability of the payment instruments, and the precise mechanics of pricing, assay rights and charges. An off-take is only as good as its weakest delivery cycle — the per-shipment mechanics deserve as much attention as the headline volume.

Key Takeaways

  • An off-take commits a buyer to purchase future production — volume certainty for both sides, with pricing floating against the LBMA benchmark.
  • Producers sign off-takes for revenue certainty and financeability; bankable off-takes underpin mine financing and prepayments.
  • Buyers secure physical supply without mine ownership, typically at a modest discount to the benchmark.
  • Each delivery usually settles like a standalone CIF transaction — letter of credit, destination assay, formula price.
  • Standby letters of credit or guarantees commonly secure the long-term commitment alongside per-shipment credits.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does an off-take agreement fix the gold price?

Rarely. Pricing almost always floats — a percentage of the LBMA fixing at each delivery — so both parties carry market price exposure but neither is betting against the other on price.

How long do gold off-take agreements run?

Anywhere from several months to the life of a mine. One to five years is common for doré supply relationships; project-finance-linked off-takes can run much longer.

Can an off-take be broken?

Only per its terms. Well-drafted agreements define remedies for missed deliveries or payments, force majeure relief and termination triggers — and security instruments give the innocent party recourse.

What discount to the gold price do off-takes carry?

It varies with the producer's circumstances, metal form and services provided. Doré formulas reflect refining charges and payable percentages; additional discounts may reflect prepayment or financing by the buyer.

Is an off-take the same as streaming or royalties?

No. An off-take is a purchase commitment for physical metal at market-linked prices. Streaming and royalty arrangements are financing structures granting rights to future production or revenue at preferential terms.
